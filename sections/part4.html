13. Governance & Engineering Standards

MethodWhy It MattersRecommendation
Dataset governancePrevents silent drift in accepted training dataVersion the dataset, registry, templates, prompts, and model choices separately
Decision logHelps the team remember why approaches were chosenTrack confirmed choices, deferred choices, and rationale in the Decision Log (Section 19)
Acceptance criteriaPrevents subjective approvals and inconsistent review qualityUse the checklist in the Execution Playbook (Section 10.5) — update as new quality issues emerge
Provenance trackingAllows every training record to be traced back to seed, template, prompt, and reviewerStore origin metadata on every accepted record
Failure analysisImproves later stages faster than only counting accepted recordsKeep a reject log with reason codes and sample examples
Promotion workflowSeparates draft candidates from trusted registry dataUse draft, validated, reviewed, accepted, and archived states

14.4 Registry Lifecycle

StatusMeaningWhen It Moves Forward
draftFreshly generated from seed, Jinja2, or LLMAfter automated validation starts
validatedPassed parser, policy, and compile checksAfter novelty and duplicate review
reviewedSeen by a reviewer and scoredAfter acceptance decision
acceptedApproved for registry useAvailable for prompting, evaluation, and later adaptation
archivedKept for history but not active for current usageWhen records are replaced or superseded
